About Peak Performance

Who We Are

Peak Performance Physical Therapy and Sports Rehab has been a leader in outpatient physical therapy services in South Florida since it first opened its doors in 2010. With nearly 10 years of proven success, we continue to put the patient first. We are dedicated to providing friendly, compassionate, professional and highly skilled treatment to the individual. Our aim is to help our patients reduce pain, improve mobility. restore function and regain quality of life. We want our patients to feel normal again.

Our Mission Statement

We are dedicated to helping our patients reduce pain, improve mobility, restore function and regain quality of life without reliance on medication. A few proven benefits of physical therapy include:

  • Less Pain
  • Improved Mobility
  • Alternative to Surgery
  • Fewer Repeat Injuries
  • Saving Money on Health Care



How Do I Get Ready For My First Visit ?

At Peak Performance, preparation is key. Here are a few tips to get you ready for your first visit.

  • First, contact the one of our two locations that suits you best.
  • Bring your filled out intake forms, physical therapy prescrition, ID and insurance card
  • Please wear loose fitting clothes
  • Be aware that the injured area will need to be exposed during the evaluation



What Do We Offer ?

We specialize in orthopedic injuries, sports injuries, recreational injuries, and rehabilitation for all ages and body parts. A few of our specialties are:

  • Cervical, thoracic, & lumbar spine
  • Acute and chronic pain
  • Post surgery & rehabilitation
  • Coordination & balance issues
  • Sports injuries & motor vehicle accidents

Meet the Peak Team

  • Alex Solano

    Clinical Director

    Alex Solano gets people moving, active and feeling young again, even after years of aches and pains. He started working with high school athletes in 2002 and then moved on to work with Division I athletes while attending California State University, Long Beach. After graduating with a Bachelor’s in Science in Athletic Training in 2006 he moved to Miami to attend Florida International University where he earned a Doctorate degree in 2009. His specialty has always been in orthopedics and sports medicine. He has extensive training in manual therapy where he has learned techniques to help identify and treat specific tissues so that the patient can move and feel better. He enjoys working with people that have a desire to get better so that they do not have to depend on a lifetime of pain pills or lose their independence. He understands that injuries can lead to functional limitations and works with patients in setting specific goals so that they create an individual plan to get better. He enjoys working with people that have undergone surgery and wants to help them restore their ability to move and become stronger again.

    A native resident of South Florida, Demond attended Dillard High School where he played football and was involved in National Honor Society activities.
